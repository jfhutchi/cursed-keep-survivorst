# Design Goals

Reward Loop Seed exists to test one loop before expanding scope. The day-one metric is restart desire: after roughly three minutes, does the player naturally want one more run?

## Fun

- The first action should be understandable within 10 seconds.
- The first pickup should happen quickly and visibly move progress.
- Upgrade choices must change the run in clear ways.
- Enemy pressure should create movement decisions without requiring complex controls.
- The result screen should make restart obvious.

## Retention

- The prototype uses free gameplay-earned randomness through upgrade offers and a free chest reveal.
- Replayability comes from different upgrade combinations and run outcomes.
- Future retention should be based on curiosity and mastery, not punishment, streak pressure, or fake urgency.

## Profitability

- Ethical future monetization can include cosmetics, premium content packs, remove-ads, DLC, or optional rewarded-ad bonuses.
- The game must remain fun with zero purchases.
- Paid randomized rewards are banned.
- Power cannot be sold through chance-based purchases.

## Discoverability / Acquisition

- The current screenshot hook is a messy arena with sparks, chasers, a visible progress meter, and an earned free chest moment.
- The 5-second video hook should be movement, auto-blasts, spark collection, and a sudden upgrade/chest reveal.
- The 15-second trailer hook should show the reward loop from danger to upgrade to chest to restart.
- Future distinctiveness needs a stronger visual/mechanical identity before art investment.

## Why One Loop Only

Multiple modes would hide whether the core loop works. This pass intentionally avoids rosters, inventory, crafting, world generation, final art, packaging, ads, purchases, and long-term systems. The only useful answer now is whether the moment-to-moment loop creates restart desire.

## Reward Rules

Free random rewards are allowed because they are earned through play and do not ask for payment. Paid randomized rewards are banned because they create exploitative incentives, unclear value, and pay-to-win pressure.
